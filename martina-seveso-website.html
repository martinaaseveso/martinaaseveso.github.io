Overview

Research

About my work

I am a Cognitive Neuroscientist at the Trinity College Institute of Neuroscience (TCIN). My research investigates how the brain integrates touch, vision, and sound to build coherent object representations — asking how we learn to categorise objects we have never encountered before.

I combine immersive VR experiments, fMRI, EEG, and physiological measures within reproducible open-science pipelines. My current postdoc focuses on neural substrates of visual and haptic categorisation across younger and older adults.

Research themes
Multisensory Object Perception & Categorisation

How does the brain combine visual, haptic, and auditory signals to form stable object categories? My work uses novel 3D objects and immersive VR to examine cross-modal transfer, categorical learning, and haptic-over-vision bias.

Neuroimaging of Object Representations (fMRI)

I design and run fMRI experiments to identify cortical regions underlying unimodal and multimodal object processing, applying RSA and GLMs to characterise how categories are encoded across age groups.

Virtual Reality as an Experimental Tool

I build Unity-based VR paradigms presenting 3D objects with simultaneous haptic, visual, and auditory feedback — enabling the study of perception beyond traditional lab constraints.

Ageing & Multisensory Perception

My current project compares how younger and older adults form and transfer object categories across modalities — asking whether ageing shifts sensory channel weighting during learning.

Physiological & Behavioural Data Science

I combine eye tracking, GSR/EDA, EEG, and fMRI data within reproducible Python and R pipelines, applying LME, survival analysis, clustering, and Bayesian inference.
